Latitude and humidity


This graph plots latitude versus humidity (to see how the latitudes were selected, see Histogram of Latitudes). Humidity percentage does not show a correlation with latitude. This means, for example, that there are cities close to the equator that have high percentages and others that have low percentages of humidity, and both record high temperatures. Although, there seems to be a higher number of cities with high percentage of humidity around the equator, this could also be said for the rest of the latitudes in the sample. Indeed, there is a numerous concentration of cities with high humidity percentage between 40° and 60° North.

So humidity does not seem to be a defining factor (1) around the equator, (2) for indicating higher temperature or (3) lower temperature.
